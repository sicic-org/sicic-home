What You'll Build¶

By the end of this programme, you will have:

  • A deployed agent running 24/7 on your own VPS infrastructure
  • A documented security architecture with sandbox isolation and identity controls
  • A domain-specific sub-agent with custom identity, skills, and workflow logic
  • A public GitHub portfolio showcasing your methodology and agent design

This is not a course about AI concepts. It is a workshop where you build real infrastructure and leave with running systems.

Who Should Apply¶

Domain Experts

Who want to productize their methodology and operate at scale without hiring a team.

Non-Technical Professionals

In law, finance, consulting, climate, or operations who already use AI daily but need to move from consumption to control.

Technical Professionals

Who want to understand the orchestration layer and build agentic systems, not just scripts.

Entrepreneurs and Solo Operators

Building company-of-one models who need leverage without headcount.

You do not need a coding background. You need clarity about what you want your agent to do, and the discipline to complete four weekly milestones.

Programme Structure¶

Week 1

Deploy

Get your agent live. By the end of Week 1, your agent is running on a VPS, connected to Discord, and responding to your commands.

Key deliverables
  • VPS setup and ZeroClaw installation
  • API key configuration and cost controls
  • Discord bot deployment with allowlist security
  • First automated task: scheduled reminder via cron

Week 2

Secure and Upgrade

Make it yours, then give it a soul. Introduce security architecture, write your agent's identity files, and build a domain-specific sub-agent.

Key deliverables
  • Sandbox configuration with forbidden paths enforcement
  • Allowlist-based identity control and audit logging
  • Root password and API key rotation
  • Workflow Design Document (200 to 400 words)
  • Nova sub-agent deployment with custom identity
  • MCP tool integration and route registration

Week 3

Document

Turn code into portfolio. Build your knowledge base, deploy a static site, and publish to GitHub Pages.

Key deliverables
  • Obsidian vault with structured documentation
  • Sanitized configuration files
  • MkDocs Material site with public manifesto
  • Live GitHub Pages URL as your proof of work

Week 4

Support and Presentation

Consolidate your learning, troubleshoot final blockers, and present your agent to the cohort. Showcase what you've built and learn from peer implementations.

Key deliverables
  • Final troubleshooting and optimisation
  • Portfolio review and refinement
  • Agent presentation to cohort (5-minute showcase)
  • Peer feedback and knowledge sharing

Frequently Asked Questions¶

Q: I'm not technical. Can I still do this?

Yes. Approximately 40% of Cohort 1 participants had no prior VPS or coding experience. The setup is guided, and you have weekly live Q&A plus community support.

Q: How much time does this require?

Plan for 5 to 7 hours per week: around 2 hours for the weekly module, around 2 hours for iteration and troubleshooting, around 1 hour for documentation, and 1 hour for the live Q&A.

Q: What if I can't attend the live sessions?

All sessions are recorded. The programme is designed for async participation. Most work happens individually on your own schedule.

Q: What happens if I don't complete all four milestones?

You can roll into a future cohort at a discounted price to complete the programme.

Q: Can I get a refund?

Refunds are available within 7 days of payment if you have not accessed any programme materials. After that, no refunds, but you can defer to a future cohort.

Q: Is this affiliated with Oxford University?

Super Individuals CIC is not directly affiliated with Oxford University. The programme is delivered independently and co-initiated by alumni from Oxford Saïd Business School and Cambridge Judge Business School.
