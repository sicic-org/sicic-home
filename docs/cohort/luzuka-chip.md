## What CHIP Does

The live site is clear about the product.

CHIP helps players turn matches into actionable improvement by:

- identifying the biggest performance gaps
- giving only 3 priority fixes
- telling the player what to focus on in the next match
- building structured 4-week improvement plans
- reviewing short clips with player-provided context

That makes the product narrow in the right way. It is not trying to be a generic sports bot. It is focused on diagnosis, prioritisation, and next-step coaching.

## How It Works

The published workflow is intentionally simple:

1. Play a match
2. Send a short description or clip with context
3. Get clear coaching guidance in seconds

The example prompt on the live page shows the kind of structured context the system expects:

- level
- side
- struggle
- goal

That is a good product choice. It keeps the input lightweight while giving the system enough signal to produce useful coaching output.

## Why It Matters

Charlotte's site frames the problem accurately:

- most players do not know what they are doing wrong
- they try to fix too many things at once
- they do not improve consistently

CHIP responds by focusing on:

- clarity
- prioritisation
- match-based learning

This is a strong founding cohort project because it takes an everyday improvement problem and turns it into a structured, decision-support workflow rather than just a content experience.

## Documentation Structure

Beyond the homepage, the live site also shows a solid documentation pattern:

- `Start Here`

That means the project is not only a demo. It is being documented as a system with product thinking behind it.
