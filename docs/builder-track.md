## What Builder Track Is For

<div class="programme-panel programme-panel--narrow">
  <p class="programme-lead">The Builder track exists for people whose questions are already moving beyond a first Telegram-to-files workflow.</p>
  <p class="programme-note">Intro track is intentionally simple. Builder track is where the architecture gets broader, the workflows get deeper, and the operational expectations get higher.</p>
</div>

## Signals You're Ready

<div class="programme-card-grid">
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>Multi-agent questions</h3>
    <p>You are already asking how to coordinate multiple agents with distinct roles instead of building one clear first agent.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>Toolchain expansion</h3>
    <p>You are comparing larger MCP ecosystems, custom toolchains, or external systems instead of only enabling the minimum useful tools.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>Automation depth</h3>
    <p>You want automation tools such as n8n or OpenCode and need to reason about when each belongs in the system.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>Operational discipline</h3>
    <p>You are thinking seriously about deployment hygiene, monitoring, reliability, and stronger production practices.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## What Builder Track Covers

<div class="programme-week-grid">
  <div class="programme-week-card">
    <p class="programme-week-number">Focus 1</p>
    <h3>Multi-agent orchestration</h3>
    <p>Move from one useful agent to systems where multiple roles, responsibilities, and handoffs need to be designed intentionally.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-week-card">
    <p class="programme-week-number">Focus 2</p>
    <h3>Broader MCP and custom tools</h3>
    <p>Work with larger MCP tool ecosystems, custom integrations, and toolchains that go beyond the small Intro track set.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-week-card">
    <p class="programme-week-number">Focus 3</p>
    <h3>Automation systems</h3>
    <p>Introduce tools such as n8n and OpenCode when the workflow genuinely benefits from richer automation and external triggers.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-week-card">
    <p class="programme-week-number">Focus 4</p>
    <h3>Deployment hygiene</h3>
    <p>Strengthen operational discipline around reliability, observability, controlled growth, and more sophisticated real-world use cases.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="programme-panel programme-panel--narrow programme-panel--soft">

## How It Relates to Intro Track

<div class="programme-card-grid">
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>Intro track teaches the core pattern</h3>
    <p>Telegram request, owned agent workflow, saved files, and publishable documentation.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>Builder track extends the pattern</h3>
    <p>More agents, more tools, more automation, more operational complexity, and more ambitious outcomes.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>The sequence matters</h3>
    <p>Starting advanced too early usually creates confusion. The Intro track foundation reduces that by making the layers understandable first.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="programme-card">
    <h3>Not a replacement</h3>
    <p>Builder track is not the beginner path. It is the continuation for people whose real growth curve starts after the basics are stable.</p>
  </div>
</div>
